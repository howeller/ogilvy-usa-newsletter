OUR ACTIONS MATTER: THE ROLE FOR BRANDS AFTER COP26
This year’s United Nations Climate Change Conference—commonly referred to as COP26—was billed as perhaps our last, best chance to avoid climate catastrophe. Ogilvy’s Sustainability Practice has broken down the key themes from the two weeks of meetings by sector and audience, identifying what it all means for brands and businesses today and over the next five years. Advertising, PR, Experience, Business Transformation and Behavioral Science will play a major role in helping drive this positive impact. The new realities of sustainability will see a greater need for behavior change, public and consumer education, and impactful strategies that convene stakeholders around ambitious climate goals. Our industry is very much part of that solution. You can use this Red Paper to engage with clients and prospects about the importance of implementing comprehensive sustainability initiatives.

Twitter Turns Real Tweet (and rejected John Lewis ad pitch) Into Christmas Spot
Back in November 2020, @DanannMcAleer tweeted a story he wrote as a seven-year-old child, complete with illustrations, claiming it was the perfect Christmas advert. He even tagged John Lewis, inviting them to use it for their 2021 campaign. John Lewis didn’t, but Twitter did. To show how the Christmas spirit floods Twitter every year, the platform brought this story to life: a surreal tale about two snowflakes, or rather ‘snow flacks’, called James and Jack, whose adventures include bouncing from an octopus in the sea into a snow spray factory.
